Honoring The Ones Who
Built The Foundation

"Their legacy is forever part of this gym."

SuperBad Boxing Gym was not built overnight. It was built through sacrifice, discipline, mentorship, and the vision of people who dedicated their lives to boxing, community, and the development of young athletes in Detroit.

✦ ✦ ✦

A Legacy Passed Down

Built Through Generations

Detroit boxing has always been more than a sport, it has been family, mentorship, survival, and opportunity. The foundation of SuperBad Boxing Gym was created by men who believed boxing could change lives and guide young people toward discipline, confidence, and purpose.

Long before championships, recognition, or titles, there were fathers, trainers, mentors, and community leaders putting in countless hours inside small gyms, teaching lessons that extended far beyond the ring.

Their vision continues to inspire everything we do today.

✦ In Memoriam
Tribute — Ali Salaam

Ali Salaam

Father · Trainer · Mentor · Builder of Champions

The Man Who Started It All

Ali Salaam was more than a boxing trainer. He was a mentor, teacher, father, and builder of champions. He dedicated his life to developing fighters, guiding youth, and teaching discipline through boxing.

As the father of Tony Harrison and L.J. Harrison, he laid the foundation that would later become SuperBad Boxing Gym. Through his leadership, sacrifices, and commitment to the sport, he helped shape not only his sons, but countless young athletes throughout Detroit.

He believed boxing was about more than fighting. It was about character, structure, accountability, and becoming stronger mentally and spiritually.

"The lessons he taught continue to live through the gym, the fighters, and the culture he helped create. Every athlete who trains at SuperBad Boxing Gym is part of the legacy he started."
✦ In Memoriam
Tribute — Coach Bass

Coach Bass

Coach · Mentor · Leader

A Coach. A Mentor. A Leader.

Coach Bass was respected for his passion, dedication, and commitment to developing fighters and mentoring young athletes. His impact reached far beyond boxing techniques and training sessions. He helped shape mindsets, build confidence, and inspire discipline in everyone around him.

Known for his wisdom, toughness, and genuine care for fighters, Coach Bass played an important role in Detroit's boxing culture and influenced multiple athletes throughout his career.

Though he is no longer with us, his energy, teachings, and influence remain part of the spirit of the gym. His contribution helped strengthen the foundation that continues to guide future generations of fighters today.
